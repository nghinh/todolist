# Product Brief: TodoList

## Executive Summary

TodoList is a personal task management app for project team members that closes the gap between "my to-do list" and "the real state of the project." Today, people's tasks live in one place while priorities, timelines, dependencies, and changes live in chats, meetings, and other tools—so they stay busy but often miss what matters most. TodoList gives each person a single place to see what to do today, why it matters, and how it ties to project goals, so daily execution stays aligned with project reality. It's a lightweight execution companion that makes alignment simple, personal, and usable every day.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Project team members manage work in a fragmented way: their personal to-do list is separate from the project's real priorities, timelines, dependencies, and changes. There is no simple way to connect daily execution to project context, so people can't reliably work on the right task at the right time with a clear sense of why it matters.

### Problem Impact

People stay busy but miss what matters; coordination depends on constant follow-up; and execution is unreliable because personal task management is disconnected from project reality. The pain is strongest for individual contributors and team leads in fast-moving, cross-functional or digital/product teams where priorities shift often and coordination is critical.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Generic todo apps focus on lists, not project context. Project tools often assume everyone lives inside one system and don't put "my list" and "project reality" in one place. The result is either no link between tasks and goals or heavy, context-switching workflows that don't fit everyday use.

### Proposed Solution

A Todo List–style app that helps each member manage daily tasks, track progress, prioritize work, and remember deadlines while staying aligned with project goals. Users get what to do today, what's most important, what's blocked, and how tasks connect to milestones—with less reliance on chats and meetings and fewer missed commitments. Execution becomes more reliable because personal task management is tied to project reality.

### Key Differentiators

- **Close the gap** between "my task list" and "the real state of the project" instead of being "just another todo app."
- **Lightweight execution companion** that combines action and context at a glance.
- **Simple, personal, and usable** so project alignment fits into everyday work.

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**Individual contributors in project teams** — Developers, designers, product people, or anyone with assigned tasks and deadlines in a shared project. They feel the problem most: their personal list is separate from real priorities and changes. They want to know what to do today, what's most important, what's blocked, and how their work ties to project goals—without digging through chats and meetings. Success is a focused day, fewer missed commitments, and feeling aligned without extra overhead.

### Secondary Users

**Team leads and project managers** — They need the team to stay aligned without constant follow-up. They benefit from everyone using a single place for tasks and context, so execution is more reliable and visibility is clearer. They may use the same app for their own tasks and to see how the team is tracking (depending on product scope).

### User Journey

- **Discovery:** Invited by team/project, or searching for "task app that ties to project" / "personal todo with project context."
- **Onboarding:** Quick sign-up, connect to project (or create one), add or import first tasks; see "what to do today" and "how this ties to the project" from day one.
- **Core usage:** Open app daily; see prioritized list, deadlines, and project context; capture and update tasks; check status and blocks with minimal context-switching.
- **Success moment:** "I knew what mattered today and didn't miss anything important" without chasing updates elsewhere.
- **Long-term:** Becomes the default place for "my work" and "how it fits the project"; less fragmentation and fewer missed commitments.

## Success Metrics

**User success — how we know we're succeeding for users**

- Users **know what to do today** and what's most important, with minimal digging in chats or meetings.
- **Fewer missed deadlines** and fewer "I didn't know that changed" moments.
- **Less time chasing updates**; task and project context are in one place.
- **"Worth it" moment:** "I knew what mattered today and didn't miss anything important" — focused execution with clear link to project goals.

**Metrics that show we're creating real value**

- **Usage:** Daily or weekly opens; use of "what to do today" / prioritized view.
- **Completion:** Tasks completed on time; tasks with status/project link.
- **Retention:** Users keep using the app as their main place for project-linked tasks.
- **Behavior:** Tasks updated in-app instead of only in chat/email; project context viewed when deciding what to do next.

### Business Objectives

- **Adoption:** Team members and teams adopt TodoList as their task + project-context tool.
- **Engagement:** Regular use (e.g. multiple times per week) and tasks kept up to date.
- **Value:** Improved execution (fewer missed commitments, less rework) and optional team-level visibility.
- **Growth:** More users and teams over time (e.g. 3–12 months), depending on go-to-market.

### Key Performance Indicators

- **User:** % of active users who open the app at least X times per week; % of tasks with a due date that are completed by due date (or marked blocked).
- **Engagement:** Tasks created/updated per user per week; use of project/milestone link or "today" view.
- **Retention:** Week-2 and month-1 retention of new users.
- **Business (if applicable):** Active teams or paid seats; NPS or "would recommend" for project task management.

## MVP Scope

**Minimum to solve the core problem:** A project team member can manage personal tasks **with enough project context to make better daily decisions**. Must include: personal task list; priority and deadline; clear "what to do today" view; simple link from each task to project goal, milestone, or deliverable; and easy task status updates so the list stays useful in real work.

**"This solves my problem":** Users open the app and immediately know what to do today, what's most important, what's at risk of being late, and why each task matters in project context. The "worth it" moment doesn't require a complex platform—only a simple, reliable experience that reduces confusion and helps them act with clarity. If the app can replace scattered notes, mental tracking, and repeated checking across chat and meetings, it's solving a real problem.

**Balancing ambition and speed:** v1 focuses on one core job: **helping individual project members turn project context into clear daily execution.** We avoid building a full project management system first. Advanced reporting, complex collaboration, team dashboards, notification logic, integrations, AI suggestions, and deep analytics are deferred. v1 priority is speed to value: lightweight, practical, and easy to adopt in daily work.

### Core Features

- Personal task list
- Task priority and deadline
- "What to do today" view
- Task status tracking
- Simple link from task to project, milestone, or goal
- Basic view of overdue and upcoming tasks

### Out of Scope for MVP

- Full team project planning
- Advanced reporting dashboards
- Complex dependency management
- Chat, comments, or heavy collaboration features
- Third-party integrations
- AI-based recommendations
- Custom workflows or industry-specific modules

### MVP Success Criteria

- Users can quickly capture and organize their tasks
- Users can identify today's priorities in seconds
- Users understand how their tasks connect to project outcomes
- Users return regularly because the app helps them stay focused and on track
- Early users report less confusion and fewer missed follow-ups

### Future Vision

Over time, TodoList can evolve from a personal execution tool into a smarter work companion that connects individual action with team coordination, project changes, and performance insights. The first version should prove one thing clearly: **a personal task tool with project context is more valuable than a standalone to-do list.**
