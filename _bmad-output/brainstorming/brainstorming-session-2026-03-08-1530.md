### First Principles Thinking (in progress)

**Key ideas from "What do we know for certain?"**

**[Foundation #1]**: Meaning, Priority, Progress, Connection  
_Concept_: Users need clarity on what to do, why it matters, when it's due, and how it connects to the bigger picture. Tasks have deadlines, priority, dependencies, and expected outcomes. The app must support capture → organize → review and help people decide what deserves attention first.  
_Novelty_: Frames the product as "alignment + execution" rather than "lists with extras."

**[Foundation #2]**: My List ↔ Project Reality  
_Concept_: Staying aligned requires more than task completion—each person must see how their work supports a team objective, milestone, or deliverable. Alignment needs context, prioritization, transparency, and regular updates because project goals are dynamic.  
_Novelty_: The core certainty is closing the gap between "my list" and "project reality," not just storing tasks.

**[Foundation #3]**: Glanceable Context, Built for Chaos  
_Concept_: "Why it matters" and "how it connects" must be low-friction and glanceable. The system must assume scattered inputs and shifting goals and still make "what deserves my attention first" and "how this ties to the project" obvious.  
_Novelty_: Design assumes chaos and re-prioritization without feeling like starting over.
